User Data Deletion Instructions

Last updated: May 2026

Seven Code Development SRL ("7code") is committed to respecting your right to erasure under GDPR Article 17 and applicable data-protection law. This page explains how to request deletion of any personal data we hold about you, and what to expect after you do.

What Data We Hold

If you submitted our PPC audit request form, we may hold:

  • Your name and work email address
  • Your website URL and monthly ad-spend range
  • Any message or challenge description you provided voluntarily
  • UTM / referral parameters logged at the time of submission
  • Meta Pixel event data associated with your browser (managed by Meta Platforms, Inc.)

How to Request Deletion

To have your data erased, send a deletion request by email:

Email: nicu.mardari@7code.ro
Subject line: Data Deletion Request
Include: the email address you used when submitting the form (so we can locate your record)

What Happens Next

  1. We will acknowledge your request within 72 hours.
  2. We will verify your identity to prevent unauthorised deletion requests.
  3. Your data will be permanently deleted from our systems and from Formspree (our form processor) within 30 days of confirmation.
  4. We will send you a written confirmation once deletion is complete.

Meta Pixel Data

Event data collected via the Meta Pixel (Pixel ID: 1066738195364104) is processed by Meta Platforms, Inc. under their own data policy. To delete or opt out of Meta's use of your data:

  • Visit facebook.com/settings → Ads
  • Use Meta's Off-Facebook Activity tool to clear and disconnect your activity

Your Rights Under GDPR Article 17

You have the right to obtain erasure of personal data concerning you without undue delay where one of the following grounds applies:

  • The data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected
  • You withdraw consent and there is no other legal ground for processing
  • You object to the processing and there are no overriding legitimate grounds
  • The data has been unlawfully processed
  • Erasure is required to comply with a legal obligation

If you believe your request has not been handled correctly, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data-protection authority.
